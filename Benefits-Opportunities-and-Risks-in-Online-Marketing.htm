Inherent advantages of the platform. These are true the moment you create an account.

Low Cost Benefit

Social media avoids the high costs of traditional media (TV/Print). Accounts are free, and ad budgets can be set as low as ¬£1.

Real World Example: Popflex / Blogilates

A small activewear brand that couldn't afford TV ads used TikTok videos to demonstrate their products. They generated millions of pounds in sales with a marketing budget of almost zero.

Targeted Audience Benefit

Platforms hold vast amounts of user data (age, location, interests), allowing businesses to show ads only to relevant people.

Real World Example: Facebook Ad Manager

Local businesses (like a driving instructor in Newport) can set their ads to appear only to people aged 16-17 who live within 10 miles of the city centre.

üîó Read: Facebook Ads Case Studies (General)

Global Reach Benefit

The internet has no borders. A local business can become an international exporter without opening physical shops abroad.

Real World Example: Gymshark

Started in a garage in the UK. By using social media influencers, they built a massive customer base in the USA and Australia before they ever opened an office there.

üîó Read: Case Study on Gymshark's Rise

Measurable Results Benefit

Digital marketing provides instant data (analytics). You know exactly how many people clicked your link.

Real World Example: Spotify Wrapped

Spotify uses user data not just for ads, but to create a viral marketing campaign that shows users exactly what they listened to, encouraging them to share the data.

üîó Read: Analysis of Spotify Wrapped Strategy
üöÄ Opportunities (The Wins)
Positive outcomes that MIGHT happen if your strategy is successful.

Viral Brand Awareness Opportunity

The algorithms can push content to millions of users who don't follow you, creating overnight fame.

Case Study: Nathan Evans (The Sea Shanty)

A Scottish postman posted a video singing "The Wellerman" on TikTok. It went viral globally, leading to a major record deal. This shows how free content can launch a career.

üîó Read: The Guardian on Nathan Evans

Market Research Opportunity

Social media allows you to ask customers directly what they want, minimising the risk of launching a bad product.

Case Study: Walkers Crisps "Choose Me or Lose Me"

Walkers asked the public to vote on classic UK flavours vs international ones. They received 770,000 votes, providing huge insight into customer preferences.

üîó Read: SLR Magazine (Results)

Community Building Opportunity

Developing a "Brand Personality" that interacts with users makes them feel loyal to the brand.

Case Study: Aldi vs M&S (#FreeCuthbert)

When M&S sued Aldi over a caterpillar cake, Aldi used humour on Twitter to mock the lawsuit. This built a massive community of supporters who cheered for the brand.

üîó Read: BBC Article on Cuthbert Wars

User Generated Content (UGC) Opportunity

Getting customers to create adverts for you. It is authentic and free.

Case Study: GoPro

GoPro's "Million Dollar Challenge" encourages users to film their own stunts. GoPro gets incredible footage to use in their TV ads without paying a camera crew.

Hazards that can damage the business, reputation, or finances.

Reputation Damage Risk

The internet is permanent. Offensive or tone-deaf posts can lead to "cancellation" and boycotts.

[Image of social media crisis management cycle]

Case Study: Burger King UK

On International Women's Day, they tweeted "Women belong in the kitchen". It was meant to be ironic (promoting female chefs), but it caused huge offence and backlash.

üîó Read: Evening Standard on Burger King Tweet

Legal Issues (Copyright) Risk

Businesses cannot use commercial music or images without a paid licence. "Fair use" rarely applies to businesses.

Case Study: Gymshark vs Sony Music

Gymshark used popular songs in their workout videos without paying for a licence. Sony Music sued them for copyright infringement, claiming damages for hundreds of songs.

üîó Read: Music Business Worldwide Article

Security & Hacking Risk

If an account is not secured (2FA), hackers can take over and post malicious content under your brand name.

Case Study: The British Army

In 2022, the British Army's Twitter and YouTube accounts were hacked. The hackers changed the profile pictures to cartoons and used the accounts to promote a Crypto scam.

üîó Read: ITV News on Army Hack

Viral Negativity Risk

You cannot control how the public uses your hashtag. They might use it to complain.

Case Study: #McDStories

McDonald's paid for a hashtag hoping for heart-warming stories. Instead, users flooded it with horror stories about food poisoning and bad service.

üîó Read: CBS News on #McDStories

üí° Exam Tip: The "So What?" Rule
